I have very few people in my life to talk with about one of my favorite things, Kingdom Hearts! It’s a series near and dear to my heart, and I love yapping about it when I get the chance to.

Kingdom Hearts is...


"Weird!"

"Pretentious!"

"Too long!"



Yup!

Kingdom Hearts is infamous for being a series of confusing, self-obsessed, time-consuming games, but what the average onlooker doesn’t know is that these games take all these blemishes in stride. A crossover between Final Fantasy and Disney is inherently silly, so the games must know exactly when to step away and wink at the player, making sure they’re in on the joke. The “pretentiousness” of the games makes for a remarkably resonant story, despite this inherent silliness. As for the length, these games use every second of their admittedly long runtimes wisely, deliberately making any grinding or backtracking optional, and rewarding everything a player might do.




But... Which One Is The Best?

It’s hard to say... but I’ll try! Below, I’ve ranked my top 5 Kingdom Hearts games, from “great” to “the best game I’ve ever played.” Of course, none of this is definitive; I implore you to try these games out for yourself!

Kingdom Hearts III

There are many things I dislike about Kingdom Hearts 3; it’s clear that Disney did a lot of meddling during the development of this game, with multiple Disney worlds tearing their cutscenes straight from the movie’s script, some of the combat systems get overwhelming, and the acting feels a little stiff at times. Still, this game is simply fun. Upon replay, the basic combat is so intricate, yet so intuitive that it takes very little effort to chain together flashy combos, even for a beginner. The higher-detail versions of locations from the PS2-era games are also beautiful, with enhanced lighting and extra NPCs to bring them to life.

Kingdom Hearts Re:coded

This game’s story is nothing to write home about, but it might have some of the best gameplay in the series. Kingdom Hearts Re:Coded is a game obsessed with variety, changing something fundamental about the core gameplay loop with every new world. One minute, you’re playing a stripped-back, arcade-y version of the Kingdom Hearts you’ve always known, and the next, you’re playing a turn-based RPG. It sounds exhausting, but this game somehow makes it work!

Kingdom Hearts

The first game in the Kingdom Hearts series is something special. It manages to be a very simple story, while perfectly laying the seeds for the games in years to come. The combat is, likewise, very simple, opting for three-hit combos rather than the flowy, flashy combos the later games are known for. Kingdom Hearts, the first one, has a charm of its own, yet to be replicated by the titles that follow it. It’s cozy, and cute, and weird, and unsettling, and mysterious, and beautiful, all at the same time.

Kingdom Hearts 358/2 Days

This is a very personal pick. Kingdom Hearts 358/2 Days (pronounced “three-five-eight days over two” for some reason) is a flawed game; the core gameplay is just as enjoyable as the games before it, but the mission-based progression can get monotonous. Where the game shines, however, is in its story, easily the best in the series. KH Days is such a strong, mature story about loss, and deception, and misguided devotion to harmful teachings, among other themes. It uses the mission-based gameplay to it advantage, drip-feeding this story over the course of several in-game days, putting the player in the main character’s shoes.

Kingdom Hearts II

This is the best game I have ever played. I love Kingdom Hearts II with my whole heart. The way this game feels to play is so elegant, with one-on-one boss fights feeling more like a dance than a swordfight. The music and the visual identity of the game are some of the best the series has ever seen. The story still makes me cry, on my tenth playthrough. The title screen makes me cry. Kingdom Hearts II is not just my favorite Kingdom Hearts game, but my favorite game.
